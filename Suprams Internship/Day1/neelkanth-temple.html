Neelkanth

Neelkanth Mahadev (Hindi: नीलकण्ठ महादेव) is a sacred temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, located about 12 kilometers from the city of Rishikesh in the Indian state of Uttarakhand. Situated in the midst of the beautiful hills of the Shivalik range, it is a popular destination for pilgrims and tourists alike.

The temple is known for its scenic views and the unique experience it offers to visitors. According to legend, Neelkanth is the site where Lord Shiva drank the poison (Halahal) during the churning of the ocean (Samudra Manthan) to save the world from its destructive effects. The poison caused Shiva's throat to turn blue, and hence the name "Neelkanth" (meaning 'blue-throated').

The temple attracts a large number of devotees, especially during the festival of Maha Shivaratri and during the months of Shravan. The Neelkanth temple is also famous for its serene atmosphere, lush green surroundings, and spiritual significance.
